Eendrachtsplein

Eendrachtsplein is a well-known square in the center of Rotterdam. The square is connected to the Westblaak, the Eendrachtsweg, the Westersingel, the Rochussenstraat, the Mauritsweg and the Oude Binnenweg. The Eendrachtsplein forms the boundary between the districts of Centrum, Cool, Dijkzigt and the Old West. The Eendrachtsplein was created as part of the Rose project in the 19th century. On the west side of the Eendrachtsplein in 1863 a special school for secondary education was built for its own account. This building has been in use since 1945 as a police station. On the east side of the square are 14 monumental buildings that were built between 1867 and 1873 to a design by D. van Ameyden van Duym. During the construction of the Westblaak in the sixties, the water of the Eendrachtsplein was filled in, with which the square lost part of its charm. As part of the second Rotterdam metro line, the Eendrachtsplein metro station was opened in 1982. At the end of the Oude Binnenweg is a statue of Koos Speenhoff, made by Adri Blok. Also since November 2008 is the notorious image 'Santa Claus' (better known as Kabouter Buttplug) by Paul McCarthy on the square. Every Tuesday there is an organic market on the Eendrachtsplein since 2005.
